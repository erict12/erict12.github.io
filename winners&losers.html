Winners & Losers

Breaking down the biggest winners and losers from this year's draft!

Winners :

New York Jets

The Jets made sure to not repeat their mistakes. They drafted their franchise QB in Wilson, and helped him out by drafting a solid guard in Vera-Tucker and a steal at receiver in the 2nd round, Elijah Moore. This draft lies on the shoulders of Wilson so only time will tell if he was the right selection.

Cleveland Browns

Cleveland shored up there lackluster secondary by drafting Greg Newsome II. He will be a great addition to a solid defensive unit. CLeveland added to their defense by trading up to draft the draft's biggest steal, Jeremiah Owusu-Koramoah as 52nd overall. JOK is undersized but has the speed and versatility to make an immediate impact in CLeveland's already stout defense.

Los Angeles Chargers

The Chargers snatched arguably the best left tackle in the draft in Rashawn Slater at 13th overall. They proceeded to select cornerback Asante Samuel Jr. to help improve their secondary. Receiver Josh Palmer selected in the 3rd round will add depth to their solid receiving crops.

Losers :

Minnesota Vikings

It is rumored that the Vikings were set to take Justin Fields at pick 14 and make him their franchise QB. Unfortunately, Chicago jumped ahead of Minnesota to snag Fields. With some no one available they loved, Minnesota traded down to get more draft capital. Darrisaw is a solid selection but the Vikings could be missing out on a great QB for the future. Minnesota selected QB Kellen Mond in the 3rd Round.

More in-depth analysis
Dallas Cowboys

The Cowboys heading into the draft desperately needed reinforcements in the secondary. Go figure the top two cornerbacks were selected right before them in successive order. This prompted Dallas to move back in the draft. The Cowboys selected Micah Parsons in the 1st round, an uber-talented linebacker who has some question marks off-the-field. Only time will tell how the draft went for Dallas but it appears that they'll miss out on a top-tier cornerback that could've helped to shore up their secondary.

More in-depth analysis
Tennessee Titans

The Titans made a risky pick by selecting cornerback Caleb Farley 22nd overall. Farley would go in the top ten if not for his injury concerns. If Farley is able to leave the injuries for the past, this pick may be the steal of the draft but the injuries are concerning enough that this is a puzzling selection when they could use good players that can contribute now. Tennessee selected a good tackle in Radunz in the second round to improve the offesnive line.
